About Community Conservation Lab

The Community Conservation Lab, located in the Hindu Kush Himalaya mountain region, promotes collaborative and inclusive heritage conservation, focusing on preserving community heritage, including buildings, At CCL enhance livelihoods, and build resilience to environmental challenges. We believe that conservation is most effective when it is locally driven, culturally relevant, and inclusive of all stakeholders.

What is Artefact?

The conservation lab manages diverse artifacts, including stories, techniques, buildings, and objects, considering their connection to human, non-human, and spiritual entities, while also acknowledging their role in natural ecology and landscape.

What is Knowledge?

Modes of knowledge production, including tacit, observational, experimental, empirical, and experiential, are valuable methods for generating and validating knowledge about artifacts and sites in conservation efforts.

Our conservation Practice?

We collaborate with local communities to practice conservation, including selecting artefacts, determining methods, documentation, condition assessment, preservation, and upkeep, learning from each other's expertise in risk assessment and decay assessment.
